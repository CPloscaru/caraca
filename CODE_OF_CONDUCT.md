## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of the Caraca community pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community. We're all here to learn, build, and have fun doing it.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without their explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all Caraca project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any project-related public spaces where an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement

All reports will be reviewed promptly and kept confidential. We are committed to fair and consistent enforcement.

**Contact:** **contact@caraca.to**

### Level 1 -- Correction

**When:** First-time or minor unintentional violations.

A private message explaining what happened and why the behavior was inappropriate. You may be asked to edit your comment or offer an apology. This is a learning moment, not a punishment.

### Level 2 -- Warning

**When:** A repeated or more serious violation.

A formal warning with restrictions on community interaction for a specified period. No contact with the involved parties during this time. Violating these terms may lead to a ban.

### Level 3 -- Ban

**When:** Sustained pattern of violations or a severe single incident.

Permanent removal from all Caraca community spaces. This is reserved for cases where other measures have failed or the behavior is egregious.
